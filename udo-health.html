UDO Health

App Design

In the summer of 2022 I just graduated from LeWagon Bootcamp in Berlin, Germany, when one of my former classmates approached me with an interesting proposition: to join him on a team of three Co-Founders with the goal of developing a mobile application in the Health Care Sector. With an outline of the project we got accepted to receive a 6-month scholarship at the Future Health Care Accelerator of BIA Berlin.

The challenge for us was to develop the best possible product as fast as possible. So we went from the initial planning including customer personas, business model canvas to user journeys, gathering feedback and ultimately designing the app in Figma, creating a working design Prototype. The idea was to create an app that lets the user keep track of their own medical data, therefore never losing any of their health related data again and being able to communicate their medical history easily with any treating physician. This would empower the individual and lead to better medical treatment in the long run.

Our Product Development Journey

The first Mockups

After wracking our brains and gathering enough data about what kind of product we wanted to build, it was clear that we wanted to build a mobile application with several core functions. Our focus was to create an app that was light and easy to use, emphasizing user experience over pure functionality and therefore creating a user experience that would invoke pleasant feelings from the user and invite the user to continuous use. This was opposed to the existing competition on the market that was overwhelmingly painted in subdued color schemes, predominantly using white, blue and green.

Using Figma I started with a simple design held in very light colors with a soft and friendly feel.

Experimenting with Iteration

From that first Design we started to gather successively more feedback from test users and iterate the Design from there. Having a clear vision of what I wanted the app to look like, while incorporating the feedback from users took a few iteration cycles and resulted in a first working Design for the UDO App.

The main challenge at this point in the Development was to bring common Design principles such as contrasting color scheme, easy to read typeface and intuitive button placement together with our image of an app, that makes Health looks cute and playful. After trying many different colors, shades and layouts slowly the final version of the app began to take shape.

Polishing the Prototype

The final and finished protoype started to take shape towards the end of our stint at BIA. Going from the initial first Design, many changes found itself incorporated into the final Design, but also some constants that I managed to keep, like the light color scheme using pink and a very light pastel blue.

The final protoype can be found on Figma for experimentation and inspiration.
